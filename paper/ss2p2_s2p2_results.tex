\section{Setup}
Both models trained and evaluated under \textbf{identical} settings, so the only difference is the
two output heads (S2P2: \emph{Proj\,\&\,Softplus} rate + rate-coupled marks; SS2P2: gated-bounded
\emph{G1} rate $\times$ rate-neutral softmax marks) on the \emph{same} latent-linear-Hawkes backbone.

\noindent\textbf{Prediction}: top-1 mark accuracy / perplexity over genuine (non-empty) events,
scored head-agnostically. \textbf{Simulation}: relative error of Cont (2001) stylized facts between a
closed-loop rollout and the real stream (lower is better); SIM is the mean of the three core facts.

\section{Reading the result}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item\textbf{Prediction: SS2P2 wins.} The rate-neutral softmax mark head reads the same backbone
embedding as S2P2's coupled head but is free to specialise, giving $+1.1$\,pp accuracy and lower
perplexity. Decoupling marks from the rate costs nothing predictively.
\item\textbf{Simulation: SS2P2 wins decisively ($\sim$6.7$\times$).} This is the whole point of the
G1 bound. S2P2's $\mathrm{softplus}(w^\top u+b)$ rate is unbounded above, so the
event\,$\to$\,state\,$\to$\,rate feedback \emph{runs away} in free-running rollout: its Fano@1s
explodes to $77.9$ (real $7.9$) and it over-clusters $5\times$. SS2P2's gated bound
$\lambda\in(\ell_-,\ell_+)$ (``Poisson sandwich'') keeps the rollout bounded — Fano $22.9$ (still high
but $3.4\times$ closer) and volatility clustering essentially exact ($0.0123$ vs real $0.0163$).
\item\textbf{Kurtosis: a wash, and a data artifact.} The real 1-second-bucket return proxy has an
enormous excess kurtosis ($\sim\!10^4$, driven by a handful of extreme buckets); \emph{neither} model
reproduces it, so kurt\_re$\approx1.0$ for both and it does not separate them.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Takeaway.} On Gemini-ETH, SS2P2 is \emph{strictly better than its S2P2 parent on both
axes}: it predicts marginally better and simulates far more faithfully, because the gated bound
converts S2P2's runaway-prone rate into a provably bounded, drift-controlled one without sacrificing
the expressive backbone or the mark head.
