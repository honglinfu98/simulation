\begin{abstract}
Neural marked temporal point processes (MTPPs) predict the next limit-order-book
(LOB) event well but \emph{simulate} order flow poorly: closed-loop, their learned
self-excitation drifts or explodes, and we show the standard remedy --- rescaling
the intensity to match the real event rate --- often has \emph{no solution} for
unbounded models. We introduce \ssppp{} (Stable State-Space Point Process), which
keeps an expressive state-space backbone verbatim and replaces only the output
heads: a \emph{softmin-bounded rate head} whose intensity carries a hard
closed-form ceiling (an exact dominating rate for thinning) with a floor of exactly
zero, and a \emph{rate-neutral} soft-max mark head that can be arbitrarily deep
without perturbing the total rate. On a strict multi-seed benchmark over three
Coinbase assets (BTC, ETH, SOL; production event rates of $24$--$48$ events/s)
with lossless streaming evaluation and \emph{verified} rate calibration for every
model, \ssppp{} is the strongest predictor on two of three assets --- it wins every
prediction metric on SOL and leads overall likelihood and marks on ETH, tying
the neural Hawkes CT-LSTM on BTC marks --- while remaining verifiably
calibratable closed-loop with competitive simulated structure.
Treating calibratability itself as an experimental outcome is the sharpest result:
the unbounded parent head fails verified calibration on 7 of 9 checkpoints ---
including all three seeds on SOL, leaving no usable simulator --- with a
near-discontinuous rate response characteristic of criticality, while the bounded
head fails once in nine. A per-gap hazard decomposition prices the residual
prediction gap exactly where the design puts it: quiet-regime hazard, the
deliberate cost of the bound. The full recipe --- bounded heads, stateful (TBPTT)
training, $O(1)$ carried-state roll-out, certificate-preserving post-hoc
calibration --- simulates at verified real rates. All tables and figures are
generated by released scripts.
\end{abstract}
