\begin{abstract}
Simulating limit-order-book (LOB) order flow is a core generative task for
market-making world models, agent training, and counterfactual analysis.
State-Space Point Process (S2P2) predicts the next LOB event
accurately, but in closed loop its learned self-excitation drifts or
explodes. We propose \ssppp{} (Stable State-Space Point Process), which keeps the
state-space backbone of \sppp{} and replaces only the output heads: a
softmin-bounded rate head whose intensity has a hard closed-form ceiling (an
exact dominating rate for Ogata thinning) and a floor of exactly zero, and a
rate-neutral soft-max mark head that can be arbitrarily deep without
perturbing the total rate. On three Coinbase assets, against five baselines
with three training seeds each and verified rate calibration for every model,
\ssppp{} is the strongest predictor on two of three assets while remaining
verifiably calibratable in closed loop with competitive simulated structure.
\ssppp{} trains with an exact parallel scan and simulates in $O(1)$ per event
by exact Ogata thinning. These results establish \ssppp{} as a 
likelihood-trained model that both predicts accurately and simulates stably,
a practical foundation for market-making world models.
\end{abstract}
