\begin{abstract}
Neural marked temporal point processes (MTPPs) predict the next limit-order-book (LOB)
event well but \emph{simulate} order flow poorly: in closed-loop roll-out their
learned self-excitation runs away, producing event rates an order of magnitude above
real and grossly distorted clustering statistics. We introduce \ssppp{} (Stable
State-Space Point Process), which keeps the expressive \sppp{} state-space backbone
\emph{verbatim} and replaces only the two output heads: a \emph{softmin-bounded rate
head} whose intensity carries a hard closed-form ceiling (an exact dominating rate for
thinning) while its floor remains exactly zero, and a \emph{rate-neutral} soft-max
mark head that can be arbitrarily deep without perturbing the total rate. Because the
backbone is unchanged, every behavioural difference is attributable to the heads. On a
corrected seven-model benchmark on Gemini ETH-USD order flow (identical training
configuration for all models), \ssppp{} comes within 0.3 nats of the best predictor
(the Neural Hawkes CT-LSTM) while holding the best timing calibration in the table,
beats its unbounded \sppp{} parent by more than 1.8 nats, and is the only
intensity-based model whose free-running roll-out is bounded by construction. An
ablation chain (two-sided bound $\to$ slow-mode initialization $\to$ open-floor
softmin head) localizes the remaining gap to the quiet regime and identifies its
mechanism --- compensator mass leaked by a welded rate floor and a frozen
zero-order-hold state asymptote --- yielding concrete, testable next steps.
\end{abstract}
