\section{Method: SS2P2}
\label{sec:method}

\ssppp{} factors the per-type intensity into a bounded total rate and a rate-neutral
mark distribution, both reading the \emph{same} backbone embedding $u(t)$:
\[
\lambda_k(t)\;=\;\lambda(t)\cdot p^*(k\mid t),
\qquad
\sum_k \lambda_k(t)=\lambda(t)\ \ \text{since}\ \sum_k p^*(k\mid t)=1 .
\]
The design principle is to put nonlinearity where it cannot perturb the rate.

\subsection{Softmin-bounded rate head}
From the LayerNorm'd (hence bounded-scale) embedding $u$, a gate produces a
two-sided-bounded state, which is read out through a smooth one-sided cap:
\begin{align}
o(u) &= \sigma(W_o u + b_o)\in(0,1)^H, &
h(u) &= o(u)\odot\tanh(u)\in(-1,1)^H,\notag\\
z_{\mathrm{raw}} &= w^{\!\top}h+b \ \ (\text{unconstrained}), &
z &= c-\mathrm{softplus}\bigl(c-z_{\mathrm{raw}}\bigr),\notag\\
\lambda(t) &= s\cdot\mathrm{softplus}(z), &&
\label{eq:softmin}
\end{align}
with a learnable positive scale $s$ initialized so the baseline rate matches a target
rate ($h\!\to\!0 \Rightarrow \lambda \approx s\ln 2$).

Two properties follow immediately from Eq.~\eqref{eq:softmin}:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Hard ceiling (simulation stability).} $z\le c$ always, hence
  $\lambda(t)\le s\cdot\mathrm{softplus}(c)$ in closed form. This is an \emph{exact}
  dominating rate for Ogata thinning --- the roll-out cannot run away, whatever state
  the closed loop visits.
\item \textbf{Open floor (prediction honesty).} As $z_{\mathrm{raw}}\to-\infty$,
  $z\to z_{\mathrm{raw}}$, so $\lambda \sim s\,e^{z_{\mathrm{raw}}}\to 0$ and
  $\log\lambda$ keeps unit gradient in the quiet regime. The floor is exactly zero.
\end{itemize}
The asymmetry is the point. Our original G1 head bounded $z$ on \emph{both} sides
(an $\ell_1$-capped readout of the gated state), giving
$\lambda\in(\ell_-,\ell_+)$ with $\ell_-\approx 0.36$\,ev/s. The ceiling
$\ell_+$ buys stability, but the nonzero floor welds the quiet regime to the burst
scale: in a quiet gap of length $\Delta t$ the compensator bleeds
$(\ell_--\lambda^{\mathrm{true}})\Delta t$ nats that no parameter setting can
recover. The requirement is asymmetric --- ceiling for simulation, zero floor for
prediction --- and the softmin cap builds exactly that asymmetry into the
nonlinearity (the ablation chain in the Experiments section quantifies the effect).

\subsection{Rate-neutral mark head}
Marks read the same $u$ but never change the total rate:
\[
p^*(k\mid t)=\mathrm{softmax}\bigl(\mathrm{MLP}(u)\bigr)_k,
\qquad
\lambda_k(t)=\lambda(t)\,p^*(k\mid t).
\]
Because the soft-max lives on the simplex, the mark network can be arbitrarily deep
--- or later conditioned on exogenous state such as an agent's resting quotes ---
without touching $\lambda$. This decouples the two failure modes: rate stability is
owned entirely by the bounded head, mark expressiveness entirely by the simplex.

\subsection{Likelihood and sampling}
Training maximizes the standard MTPP likelihood with the compensator evaluated on the
training window; the mark term is a categorical cross-entropy on $p^*(k_i\mid t_i)$,
decoupled from the rate term. Closed-loop sampling uses thinning with the exact
dominating rate $s\cdot\mathrm{softplus}(c)$. Two further training-side options are
kept from the benchmark harness: an S4-style log-spaced initialization of the
backbone decay spectrum (timescales $0.02$--$60$\,s), and an unbiased Monte-Carlo
compensator estimate in place of the endpoint rule (\texttt{--mc-compensator});
the ablation chain evaluates the former, and the latter is identified as the
companion lever for the residual rate inflation shared by all windowed-trained
intensity models.
