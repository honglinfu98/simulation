\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We showed that the prediction--simulation tension in neural LOB models is a property
of the output heads, not the backbone. Keeping the \sppp{} state-space backbone
verbatim and transplanting a softmin-bounded rate head and a rate-neutral mark head
yields \ssppp: within 0.3 nats of the best predictor, best-in-table timing
calibration, $\ge$1.8 nats better than its unbounded parent, and a roll-out bounded
by a hard closed-form ceiling that doubles as an exact thinning rate. The ablation
chain turns the remaining gap into mechanism: a welded rate floor (fixed by the
softmin head) and a frozen ZOH state asymptote plus endpoint-compensator bias (the
identified next levers: leaky hold and Monte-Carlo compensator). Because the mark
head is rate-neutral, conditioning it on exogenous state --- an agent's resting
quotes --- cannot destabilize the rate, making \ssppp{} a suitable environment model
for market-making world models; that action-conditioned extension is future work.
