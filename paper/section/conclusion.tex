\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We decomposed the prediction--simulation tension in neural LOB models into
independent causes and resolved each with a single-factor ablation. The \ssppp{}
heads (softmin-bounded rate $\times$ rate-neutral marks, on an unchanged \sppp{}
backbone) supply stability and exact samplability: within 0.3 nats of the best
predictor, $\ge$1.8 nats better than the unbounded parent, provably bounded
roll-out. Carried-state simulation removes the memory ceiling that made long-range
volatility clustering unreachable; stateful (TBPTT) training improves prediction
across every metric and brings simulated temporal structure to near-real levels; and
the factorized head admits an exact, certificate-preserving post-hoc rate
calibration that closes the remaining rate gap --- and, by repairing the clock-rate
distortion, improves every structure fact. The combined recipe --- bounded heads,
stateful training, carried-state roll-out, calibrated scale --- simulates at
near-real rates with close-to-empirical clustering and return-autocorrelation while
remaining exactly samplable end-to-end. Alongside, a cautionary negative result: the
textbook unbiased Monte-Carlo compensator collapses training under clipped adaptive
optimization despite verified unbiasedness --- estimator variance governs whether
likelihood-level calibration succeeds, and a stratified per-gap estimator is the
indicated repair and our next experiment. Because the mark head is rate-neutral,
conditioning it on exogenous state --- an agent's resting quotes --- cannot
destabilize the rate, making \ssppp{} a suitable environment model for market-making
world models; that action-conditioned extension is future work.
