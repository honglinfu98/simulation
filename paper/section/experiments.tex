\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Protocol}
\label{sec:setup}
We benchmark on Coinbase BTC, ETH, and SOL event-driven order flow, seven days
per asset (validation-split rates $38.3$, $47.9$, and $24.0$ events/s), encoded
by the TFOW event constructor \citep{fu2026tfow}. Each atomic event type is a
triple $e=(c,s,\delta)\in\mathcal{E}$: an event class
$c\in\{\mathrm{LO},\mathrm{MO},\mathrm{CO},\mathrm{IS}\}$ (limit order,
market order, cancel order, inside-spread order), a side $s\in\{b,a\}$, and a
level index $\delta$. For LO/CO, $\delta\in\{1,\dots,K\}$ is the distance
from the best quote over the top $K{=}10$ book levels per side; for MO,
$\delta=1$ (execution against the standing best); for IS,
$\delta\in\{1,\dots,K\}$ is the number of ticks by which the new quote
improves the spread. This yields $|\mathcal{E}|=62$ atomic types
($20+20+2+20$), classified deterministically from synchronized Level-2 book
snapshots and trade records by a three-question decision tree (trade at the
touch $\to$ MO; liquidity drop without trade $\to$ CO; liquidity added inside
the spread $\to$ IS; otherwise LO). Simultaneous atomic events at one
timestamp form an event set $X_{t_i}$; we model the resulting type stream with
categorical marks and leave volume marks $v(e)$ unmodeled. Six models --- \nhp{} (the Mei--Eisner CT-LSTM), an LSTM decoder and
a SAHP-style causal-attention decoder \citep{zhang2020self} (both \emph{adapted
generic backbones} over our shared input embedding, not paper-faithful
re-implementations), PCT-LSTM (a per-type parallel CT-LSTM), \sppp{}, and
\ssppp{} --- are trained under an identical configuration per asset: sequence
length 1024, non-overlapping windows, identical gradient-step counts, three
training seeds each. The fixed 1024-event context spans only ${\sim}20$--$40$\,s
of market time at these rates, so long-memory structure leans entirely on
carried state. The protocol is strict end-to-end:

\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{1pt}
\item \textbf{Prediction} is scored on \emph{every} genuine test event by a
  lossless streaming evaluator: raw test segments are traversed in stream order
  with recurrent state carried across chunks (one cold start per genuine segment
  boundary); the evaluator hard-fails if any event goes unscored.
\item \textbf{Simulation} uses carried-state closed-loop roll-outs
  ($32\times600$\,s, three roll-out seeds per checkpoint) compared to
  equal-duration bootstrap samples of real segments.
\item \textbf{Every model is rate-calibrated} before scoring: a one-dimensional
  bisection on a time-rescaling constant $\kappa$ (mark-preserving for every decoder;
  see Method) targets the \emph{validation}-split rate, then a full-scale
  validation roll-out must verify the target within 15\%; failures escalate to
  full-scale probes and, if still unmet, the checkpoint is excluded and the
  failure reported as a result. SAHP is the exception: its closed-loop rate is
  unstable across warm-starts and no constant $\kappa$ transfers, so it is reported
  uncalibrated ($k{=}1$, marked \dag).
\item \textbf{Statistics}: roll-out seeds are averaged within each checkpoint;
  95\% CIs are $t$-based across checkpoints. Any batch failure or non-finite
  value aborts the run.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Prediction, and where its nats live}
\label{sec:prediction}
Table~\ref{tab:multiasset}: \ssppp{} is the strongest predictor on two of three
assets --- it wins overall NLL and accuracy outright on ETH ($-0.87$ vs.\ \nhp's
$-0.81$; $0.345$ vs.\ $0.341$) and on SOL by a wide margin ($0.27$ vs.\ $0.53$;
$0.192$ vs.\ $0.184$), and on BTC ties \nhp{} on marks while conceding overall
likelihood. The concession is timing, and a per-gap decomposition of
timeNLL $= -\log\lambda(t_i) + \Delta u_i$ (event term $+$ compensator mass)
locates it precisely on BTC, the asset where \nhp{} leads. Burst sharpness is
\emph{not} the issue: the state-space family's event terms are better than
\nhp's ($-4.95$ \ssppp{} vs.\ $-4.23$), and intensity-ceiling saturation is
comparable across models (${\sim}25\%$). The entire concession is compensator
mass in \emph{quiet gaps}: mean per-gap rescaled mass is $1.88$ (\ssppp) and
$2.26$ (\sppp) against \nhp's near-ideal $1.03$ (target $1$), with the excess
accruing in gaps beyond $0.1$\,s --- rare at $38$\,ev/s, but expensive
(\ssppp{} integrates $10.0$ units of hazard across a $0.5$--$2$\,s gap where
\nhp{} integrates $6.0$; \sppp{} $16.4$). Figure~\ref{fig:hazard} shows why:
\nhp's per-dimension decay toward a \emph{learned steady state} tracks the
falling empirical hazard of real gaps, while the state-space family --- whose
quiet-state behaviour is an emergent readout of decaying latents with no
dedicated resting-rate parameter --- stays hot through quiet gaps. This cost is
the deliberate price of stability; what it buys is the subject of the next two
subsections.

\subsection{Simulation}
\label{sec:simulation}
Table~\ref{tab:multiassetsf} and Figure~\ref{fig:forest} score calibrated
closed-loop roll-outs. Every calibrated model lands its rate (rel-err
$0.06$--$0.42$); the discriminating columns are structural.
PCT-LSTM leads the Fano column on all three assets; clustering splits
between \nhp{} (BTC, ETH) and \ssppp{} (SOL); \ssppp{} is competitive on
every structural column of every asset --- the only model in the table that
combines that with top-tier prediction --- while the LSTM sampler pins Fano
rel-err at ${\approx}0.99$ throughout: it simulates a near-Poisson stream, the
trivially-stable end of the frontier. Figure~\ref{fig:fano} shows the
level-and-rise view on BTC: real Fano rises from ${\sim}60$ at 1\,s to
${\sim}1000$ at 50\,s; every model under-disperses at this context length,
PCT-LSTM and \ssppp{} least. The one surviving \sppp{}
arm on ETH calibrates in rate but over-disperses wildly (Fano rel-err $6.0$)
--- rate calibration cannot repair a near-critical checkpoint's structure.
\nhp{}, the best BTC predictor, is mid-pack in simulation on every asset:
prediction and simulation quality remain distinct axes, and no unbounded model
wins both.

\subsection{Calibratability is a model property}
\label{sec:calibratability}
Post-hoc rate calibration (Method) is mark-preserving for \emph{every} decoder
in the zoo --- a constant $\kappa$ rescales sim-time intensity without touching type
probabilities --- so we calibrate every model, and treat the \emph{outcome} of
calibration as an experimental result in its own right. The protocol is
deliberately falsifiable: probes must bracket and converge to the validation
target, and a full-scale validation roll-out must verify it; every failure is
reproducible (probe seeds are deterministic, and each retried failure
reproduced its bisection bracket bit-identically).

Table~\ref{tab:caloutcomes} is, to our knowledge, the first cross-asset
quantification of closed-loop calibratability for neural TPPs: at production
event rates, the unbounded head is uncalibratable on 7 of 9 checkpoints, and on
SOL the model class is excluded outright. Figure~\ref{fig:ladder} shows the
failure signature: the closed-loop rate response of a failing \sppp{}
checkpoint is near-discontinuous in $\kappa$ --- a $10^{-3}$ change in $\kappa$ flips the
realized rate across the entire tolerance band (e.g.\ $36\to48.5$\,ev/s), and
seed-to-seed variance at fixed $\kappa$ reaches ${\sim}30\%$ --- the behaviour of a
system parked near criticality, where no constant time-rescaling exists. The
bounded \ssppp{} response crosses the same target smoothly and verifies on
eight of nine checkpoints, including all three ETH seeds; its single failure
is qualitatively different --- a per-lane diagnostic traces it to a
\emph{metastable} burst-locked regime at ${\sim}2\times$ the target rate that
individual roll-out lanes enter stochastically, bounded and benign next to
\sppp's criticality, and likely passable under a seed-averaged probe. This is the
certificate made empirical: the same unbounded softplus head whose
intensity-collapse states produce \sppp's pathological expected-time tail
(Table~\ref{tab:multiasset}) also parks checkpoints near criticality
closed-loop, and both pathologies disappear under the softmin bound.
