\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Neural TPPs for LOB streams.}
Neural MTPPs \citep{du2016recurrent,Mei2017TheProcess,zhang2020self,Shchur2021NeuralReview}
learn history-dependent intensities; \citet{Shi2022StateSimulation} specialize them
to LOB event streams with a state-space backbone evaluated on both prediction and
simulation --- the backbone we adopt verbatim. Our contribution is orthogonal to the
encoder line: we change what is read \emph{out} of the state, showing the heads, not
the backbone, own the stability/calibration behaviour.

\paragraph{Hawkes modeling of order flow.}
Parametric Hawkes processes are the classical LOB event model
\citep{Hawkes1971,Hawkes2018HawkesReview,Jain2024LimitProcess,jain2024review},
with state-dependent extensions \citep{Morariu-Patrichi2022State-dependentModelling};
their linear structure gives exact stationarity and branching-ratio certificates but
limited expressiveness. \ssppp{} takes the complementary route: full neural
expressiveness with stability restored by a closed-form bound rather than by
linearity.

\paragraph{LOB simulators and their evaluation.}
Agent-based and generative LOB simulators
\citep{byrd2020abides,coletta2021lobgan,ElwinSimulatingMicrostructure} are typically
scored on stylized-facts realism \citep{cont2001empirical,vyetrenko2020getreal}. We
follow that protocol but insist on a single model family scoring \emph{both} axes ---
per-event predictive likelihood and closed-loop realism --- since it is the tension
between them that defines the frontier.

\paragraph{Bounded intensities.}
Bounding or normalizing intensity functions for tractable sampling appears in the
TPP literature mainly as a computational device (e.g., dominating rates for
thinning). Here the bound is a modeling decision with an asymmetry requirement:
a hard ceiling for closed-loop stability, an exactly-zero floor for quiet-regime
likelihood --- and the softmin cap realizes precisely this pair.
